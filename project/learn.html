How to Play Chess

YouTube video by Gothamchess

Chess is a strategic board game for two players, where each player controls a set of 16 pieces of 6 types on a chessboard. Every piece has its own unique way of moving. The primary objective is to achieve checkmate, which happens when the opponent's king is in the position of capture without any possible escape. Apart from checkmate, games can conclude in different ways, including resignation by a player or a draw, which can be achieved through various means.

  • Two-player game:

    Chess is played between two players, referred to as White and Black. Each player controls a set of chess pieces in their respective colors. Typically, White moves first in chess. This is determined either by mutual agreement or by a random method such as flipping a coin.

  • Six types of pieces:

    The chessboard starts with each player having 16 pieces, which include the king, queen, rooks, bishops, knights, and pawns. Each type of piece has its own unique way of moving across the board.

  • Checkmate:

    The ultimate goal of the game is to checkmate the opponent's king. Checkmate occurs when the king is under attack (in check) and there is no legal move to remove the threat. If a player checkmates their opponent, they win the game.

  • Other ways to end the game:

    Besides checkmate, there are several ways a game can end:

    • Resignation
    • Draw
    • Agreement

Understanding and adhering to the rules of chess is crucial for fair play and effective competition. It ensures that the game is played in a structured manner and allows for consistent evaluation of positions and outcomes.
